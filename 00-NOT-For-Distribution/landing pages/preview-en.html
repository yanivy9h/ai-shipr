The System

What is AI-SHIPR

AI-SHIPR is a Product Operating System built inside Obsidian and Claude Code. Not a note-taking system. Not a template library. A structured model for how you think, decide, and act as a PM — with AI as your operating layer.

The system knows your product, your strategic bets, your active initiatives, and your open hypotheses. You do not describe your work to AI every session — the system already has context. You just tell it what is happening right now.

It covers the full Agile loop — not just the planning phase.

Inside AI-SHIPR

Four agents. Fourteen skills. Eight playbooks.

Not a single AI chat — a team of specialized agents, each holding a different part of your product context, equipped with skills they can run on demand.

The Agents

S

Strategy Agent

Holds your product vision, strategic bets, and long-horizon decisions. Pull it in whenever a decision needs product-level context.

D

Discovery Agent

Turns new ideas into structured hypotheses. Maps assumptions, scores risk, and converts the most dangerous one into something testable.

L

Delivery Agent

Manages your sprint context, active initiatives, and daily priorities. Runs the morning brief. Always knows what is in motion.

K

Stakeholder Agent

Preps meeting agendas, drafts updates, and keeps open decisions visible so nothing gets lost between conversations.

Questions

Do I need to know how to code?

No. AI-SHIPR runs in Obsidian (a writing app) and Claude Code (an AI terminal). You write in plain text and markdown — no code involved. If you can use Notion, you can use this system.

What do I need to install before the session?

Claude Code (free CLI from Anthropic), Obsidian (free note app), and the AI-SHIPR starter kit (sent 48 hours before). Setup takes about 20 minutes. You arrive with the system already running.

Is this a recorded course I can watch later?

No — it is a live, hands-on session. The value is in running the system yourself with real-time feedback, not watching a recording.

I have already used Claude. Will this still be useful?

Almost certainly yes. Most PMs who use Claude get inconsistent results because they are prompting without a persistent structure. AI-SHIPR gives Claude permanent product context. The difference in output quality is significant.

How is this different from other AI-for-PMs content?

Most content teaches isolated workflows for individual tasks. AI-SHIPR is a full operating model — morning brief, new idea intake, sprint kickoff, 1:1 prep, retrospectives, and more. It is not a prompt collection. It is the architecture that connects everything.

What happens after the workshop?

You get The Half-Sprint Guide — a 5-day plan to migrate the system from the example product to your real product. Within a week you will have your first morning brief running on your own initiatives.

What if I cannot make it?

Reach out before the session date and we will move you to the next one. The goal is that you leave with something working.
