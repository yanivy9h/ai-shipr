The three ideas to land

Idea 1 — CLAUDE.md is the session start protocol

Every time Claude Code opens this folder, it reads CLAUDE.md first. That file is the contract between you and the AI. It tells the AI who you are, what the system is, and what to do before responding to anything.

"Think of CLAUDE.md as the brief you give to a new team member before they start. Except this team member forgets everything between sessions — so you brief them every single time. That's what CLAUDE.md does. It's not documentation. It's the handshake."
Idea 2 — Settings.md controls behavior

Three settings matter today: voice (direct / coach / gentle), pm_voice (on/off), and integrations. Show each one. Voice changes how the AI responds — direct gives you conclusions first, no ceremony. Coach asks you questions back.

"The voice setting is one of the most important things in the whole system. Different situations need different modes. Before a sprint planning: direct. When you're working through a hard problem: coach. Before a sensitive stakeholder conversation: gentle. You set it once in Settings.md, and every agent and skill will apply it."
Idea 3 — The folder structure is the system

Walk through each folder in 30 seconds each:

  • S-Strategy — the strategic context (Vision, KPIs, Bets). Nothing runs without this.
  • I-Initiatives — your active bets, with stages, hypotheses, and open decisions
  • H-Hypotheses — every assumption turned into a falsifiable test
  • P-Proof — experiments and results
  • R-Relationships — your PM profile, stakeholders, meeting log, and voice
  • AI/ — where all the agents, skills, workflows, and playbooks live
  • Learning.md — the loop memory. This is where the system compounds.
"The folder structure is not bureaucracy. It's the PM's operating model. Everything you do as a PM — deciding what to build, aligning stakeholders, testing assumptions, running sprints — maps to a folder. If it doesn't fit the structure, it probably isn't product work."
At 13 minutes: start wrapping up. Don't over-explain. The demo in Phase 2 is more powerful than any explanation.

Walk-through script

1. The product — 30 seconds
"CourtIQ is a basketball drill training app for players aged 10–18. Players pick a drill, watch a video, do the reps. Coaches can assign drills. Parents track progress and pay the subscription. Simple product. Real retention problem. You are the PM for the next 35 minutes."
2. Vision.md — 1 minute
"Here's the Vision file. One sentence: CourtIQ gives young basketball players access to professional drill videos they can train with on their own and track their improvement over time. Notice what's also in this file — what we will NOT do. No coaching platform. No team rosters. Basketball only through 2026. A vision without constraints is just a wish. These constraints make decisions faster."
3. KPIs.md — 1 minute
"North Star: Weekly Drill Completions. Currently 3,200 — target 8,000 by Q3. The four supporting metrics tell a story: Week-2 Retention is 31% — we lose 70% of new users before the habit forms. Coach-Activated Players is zero right now — that's a channel we haven't opened yet. Parent Renewal is 58% — parents cancel because they can't see progress. These numbers are the system's operating context. Every agent that runs will see these."
4. PM-Profile.md — 1 minute
"This is the PM's profile. Notice the biggest challenge section: three competing hypotheses for why retention drops, can't test all of them at once, needs to sequence experiments intelligently. And the AI-SHIPR leverage section — faster decision framing, better 1:1 prep with Omer, stakeholder-ready outputs. This file tells the AI who it's working with and what good output looks like for this person."
5. INI-001 — 1.5 minutes
"Short-form drill videos. Stage: In Sprint. Look at the outcome — average session length drops below 4 minutes, drill completion rate holds or improves versus 38% baseline. There's a live A/B test running right now — EXP-001, started February 24th, day-14 read on March 10th. There's also an open question still unresolved: should short-form be a separate track or replace existing videos entirely? That's a real decision the PM hasn't made yet. The system holds it until we're ready."
6. HYP-001 + EXP-001 — 1.5 minutes
"HYP-001: short videos increase daily drill completion rate by 40%. Status: Testing. EXP-001 is the A/B test that validates or kills it — five Ball Handling Foundation drills reformatted to under 2 minutes. Look at the failure threshold. If we don't see a 40% lift in completion rate by day 14, the hypothesis is wrong and we reassess. This is what hypothesis discipline looks like in practice. Not 'we think shorter videos will work.' 40% lift. 14-day window. Specific failure condition."
Don't read all the content. Point, highlight the key fields, move on. The goal is to show the structure and the thinking behind it — not to explain CourtIQ's business. They can read the files themselves after the session.

"The Half-Sprint-Guide is the activation plan. You don't build AI-SHIPR all at once. You build it in 5 focused sessions. Day 1: strategy setup. Day 2: your first initiative. Day 3: your first hypothesis. Day 4: run your first workflow. Day 5: close the loop with a weekly review. By Friday, you have a working system. Not perfect. Working."

The 5-day plan

Day Task Time
Day 1 Fill in S-Strategy (Vision, KPIs, 2–3 Bets). Run Populate-Strategy workflow. 60 min
Day 2 Create your first initiative file. Link to a bet. Set Stage. Add one open decision. 45 min
Day 3 Run Assumption-Extractor on your initiative. Convert top assumption to a hypothesis with Hypothesis-Builder. 45 min
Day 4 Run /today. Should give a real brief now. Run 1on1-Prep for your next stakeholder meeting. 30 min
Day 5 Run /weekly. Close the loop. Update Learning.md. 30 min
Q&A — 10 minutes

Open the floor. Common questions and suggested answers:

Common questions

Q: What if I have too many initiatives?
"That's a real product problem, not an AI-SHIPR problem. The system will surface it — /today will show you 6 active initiatives and the brief will be unusable. That's feedback. Pare back. AI-SHIPR works best when you have 2–3 active initiatives, each with 1–2 hypotheses. If you have more than that, you have a prioritisation problem, not a system problem."
Q: How long does it take before the system actually helps me?
"Day 4. Seriously — after the 5-day plan, /today becomes a real brief. Before that you're setting it up. The setup is not wasted time. Filling in Vision and KPIs is product work you should be doing anyway. AI-SHIPR just makes you do it in a structured way and stores the output in a format the AI can use."
Q: What about teams — can multiple PMs use this?
"Yes, with some setup. AI-SHIPR is designed for a single PM by default — one PM-Profile, one voice. For teams, the recommended pattern is one shared S-Strategy folder (owned by the lead PM) and separate I-Initiatives folders per squad. We cover this in the follow-up workshop. For now, start with your own system. Get it working for you first."
Closing — 3 minutes
"You have a working system in your hands. It's not magic — it's structure. The AI is only as good as the context you give it. But when you give it good context — real strategy, real initiatives, real hypotheses — the output stops being generic advice and starts being a brief for your specific situation."
