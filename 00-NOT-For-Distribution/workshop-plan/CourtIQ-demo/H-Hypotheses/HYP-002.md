# HYP — Visible progress indicators (streak + drill history) improve week-2 retention by 25%

## Related Initiative
- [[Initiative: Progress tracking — visible improvement]] — INI-002

## Risk Level
High

## Why this might fail
- 14-year-olds may not be motivated by streaks — that's an adult productivity behaviour pattern. Ben wants to be better at basketball, not maintain a streak counter.
- Progress may feel meaningless if there's no benchmark — "I completed 8 drills" doesn't tell Ben if that's good or bad
- The retention problem may be upstream of the progress screen — if players don't complete enough drills to have meaningful progress to show, a progress screen has nothing to display
- Parents (Mira) might not see the progress screen at all unless we actively push it to them via email — the feature exists but doesn't solve her problem without a notification layer

## Test Plan
- Method: Show progress screen (streak + drill history) to 30% of new users starting in week 1. Control group sees no progress screen.
- Who/where: New users only. Both Ben-type and Mira-type users included — we want to see if the effect holds across both.
- Duration: 21 days (to measure week-2 return and early week-3 signal)

## Metric + Threshold
- Metric: Week-2 retention rate (user returns and completes at least 1 drill in days 8–14)
- Success threshold: +25% improvement vs. control (e.g. 31% → 39%+)
- Failure threshold: Less than +10% improvement — progress screen alone is not the retention lever
