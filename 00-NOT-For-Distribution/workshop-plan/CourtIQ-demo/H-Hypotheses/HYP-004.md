# HYP-004 — Language Frame in Re-Engagement Notifications Drives Higher Player Return

**Full statement:**
Players who receive a notification with an achievement frame ("You're 1 drill away from a streak") or a directive frame ("Today's drill is ready — 3 minutes") on day 3 after signup will return and complete a drill at a higher rate than players who receive the current neutral copy ("Time to practice your basketball drills").

**Related initiative:** INI-002 (Progress tracking — visible improvement)
**Risk level:** Low-Medium

**Why this might fail:**
- Day-3 churn may already be decided before the notification arrives — no copy recovers fully disengaged players
- Notification open rates may be below 25% — if the notification isn't opened, the language doesn't matter
- Achievement frame may create pressure and backfire, especially for younger players who haven't completed any drills yet
- Directive frame may feel condescending for players who already know what to do

**Signal from interviews:**
- INT-001 (Noam, active Ben-type): "beat your own number" — achievement/competitive frame resonates
- INT-002 (Tal, churned Ben-type): "if it told me what to do… do this today" — directive frame is what he needed

**Test plan:** EXP-002

**Metric + Threshold:**
- Metric: Day-3 return rate (% of notified players who open app + complete ≥1 drill within 24h)
- Success: +20% vs. control
- Failure threshold: <+10% across both treatment arms

**Status:** Untested
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-05
