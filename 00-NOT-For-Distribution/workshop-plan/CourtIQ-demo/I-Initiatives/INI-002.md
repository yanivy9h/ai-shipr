# Initiative: Progress tracking — visible improvement

## Strategic Link
- [[ABC-TOM/AI-SHIPR/S-Strategy/Strategic-Bets]]: Bet 2 — Progress tracking that shows visible improvement
- Why this bet now: Week-2 retention is 31%. Churn interviews show players don't return because they can't see they're improving. Parents cancel subscriptions because they have no evidence of value. Progress visibility addresses both problems.

## Outcome (must be measurable)
- Metric: Week-2 retention rate
- Desired change: From 31% to 50% within 60 days of launch
- Time horizon: Q3 2026
- Secondary metric: Parent renewal rate — target 75% (from 58%)

## Linked Hypotheses
- [[HYP-002]] — Visible progress indicators improve week-2 retention by 25%
- [[HYP-003]] — Coach-assigned drills drive 3x player completion (feeds into whether progress visibility works differently for coach-activated players)

## Decision Needed?
Yes
- Decision: Which metric to surface first on the progress screen — streak counter, drill history, or improvement graph?
- Decision by: 2026-03-10
- Decision criteria: Which metric is (a) technically feasible in 1 sprint and (b) most likely to drive return behaviour?
- Options considered:
  1. Streak counter — easiest to build, proven in habit apps (Duolingo), but kids may find it pressuring rather than motivating
  2. Drill history — shows what you did, not improvement — lower motivational pull, but easier to parse
  3. Improvement graph — most compelling for Mira, highest motivational pull, but requires data aggregation layer (3-sprint effort per Ron)
