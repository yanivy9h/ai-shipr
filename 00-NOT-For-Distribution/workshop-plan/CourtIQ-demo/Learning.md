## Log

### 2026-02-12 — Onboarding videos don't drive sustained retention

**Source:** Experiment EXP-002 (7-day A/B test, new user onboarding flow)

**What happened:** Added a 3-video onboarding sequence explaining how to use the app. Day-7 retention improved by 8% vs. control. Day-14 retention was flat.

**Implication:** Onboarding polish helps early engagement but doesn't fix the habit loop. Users understand the app — they just don't have a reason to come back. The problem is not comprehension, it's the absence of a pull mechanism after the first session. Next hypothesis to test: progress visibility as the return trigger.

---

### 2026-02-24 — Parents are cancelling because they can't see their kid's progress

**Source:** 6 churn interviews with cancelled subscribers (Mira-type personas, all parents)

**What happened:** All 6 cancelled subscriptions in month 1 were parents paying on behalf of their kids. When asked why: "I didn't know if Ben was actually using it." None of them had access to a progress view. All 6 said they would have stayed if they could see completion data.

**Implication:** Progress visibility for parents may be as important as progress visibility for players. Mira is the economic buyer — she controls the renewal decision even though she never opens the app. A parent-facing progress summary (weekly email or dashboard) could move renewal rate independently of player engagement. Investigate whether parent visibility is a separate initiative or a feature of INI-002.
