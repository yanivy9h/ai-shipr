# PM Voice Profile

_Generated from writing samples — Dana Avraham, CourtIQ PM_

---

## Voice in one sentence
Precise and direct — leads with the recommendation, then the reasoning, never the other way around.

## Sentence structure
Short to medium sentences. Avoids subordinate clauses where a new sentence works just as well. Writes how she talks. Doesn't over-explain things that are obvious to the reader.

## How messages open
Straight to the key information or the decision needed. Never "Just wanted to loop you in on..." or "Hope this finds you well." The first sentence carries the payload.

## Decision framing
States a clear recommendation first, then lists 2–3 alternatives with real tradeoffs — not fake balance. Doesn't ask for opinions on decisions she's already made. Asks for input on things that are genuinely open and where the other person's perspective would change the call.

## How disagreement sounds
"I hear you, and I want to push back on one thing." Uses "and" not "but" to connect disagreement to common ground. Acknowledges the other person's point before redirecting. Doesn't soften the disagreement, but never punishes it either.

## Closing style
Always ends with a clear next step or a specific question. Never "let me know your thoughts." Usually: "Does this work for Thursday?" or "I'll move forward with this unless I hear back by EOD Wednesday."

## Vocabulary fingerprint
Used often: "tight", "signal", "directionally", "what's the bet here?", "I want to flag", "move the needle", "this is the riskiest assumption"
Never uses: "synergy", "circle back", "per my last email", "just wanted to", "touch base", "hopefully", "as per"

## Emotional register
Calm under pressure. Doesn't catastrophize. Acknowledges uncertainty without dramatizing it. In writing: measured, occasionally dry humour, never passive-aggressive. Respects the reader's time — cuts anything that doesn't need to be there.

---

## Usage note for agents
When generating written outputs for Dana (stakeholder updates, 1:1 agendas, decision logs, sprint summaries), apply the patterns above. Lead with the recommendation. Keep sentences tight. End with a clear next step. Do not make the output sound like generic AI — if Dana reads it and thinks "I wouldn't have written it this way," it failed.
