# Stakeholders

## Omer Levi — CEO

- Optimizes for: MAU growth and revenue signals that support the Series A conversation in 6 months. Thinks in terms of headline metrics and investor narrative.
- Biggest fear: Burn rate outpaces traction — raises the next round from a position of weakness or can't raise at all.
- Communication style: High-level and fast. Doesn't want to hear about feature details — wants to know the impact on the numbers and when it ships. Thinks out loud and expects quick reactions.
- Common objections: "Is this the fastest path to growth?" / "Can we do this in less time?" / "What's the upside if it works?"
- What he needs to say yes: A clear link between the initiative and MAU or revenue, a realistic timeline, and confidence that the team isn't distracted from higher-leverage work.

---

## Tamar Gold — Head of Product

- Optimizes for: Strategic clarity and product quality. Wants the team making decisions for the right reasons, not just shipping things fast.
- Biggest fear: The team builds a roadmap of features that individually make sense but don't compound into a coherent product. Feature factory mode.
- Communication style: Structured and data-driven. Likes frameworks and clear hypotheses. Will always probe the "why" before engaging on the "what." Not satisfied with "we think this will work" without a testable mechanism.
- Common objections: "Have we validated this?" / "What's the hypothesis?" / "How will we know if this worked?"
- What she needs to say yes: Hypothesis clearly stated, experiment plan defined before build starts, user signal cited (not just intuition), clear success and failure criteria.

---

## Yael Shapiro — Product Marketing Manager

- Optimizes for: Activation rates, messaging that converts, and launch readiness. Measures her success on whether new users understand the product and stick around past day 3.
- Biggest fear: Product ships and she finds out two days before launch. No story, no launch assets, no time to prepare. Has been burned by this before.
- Communication style: Collaborative and curious. Thinks in customer narratives and personas. Asks a lot of questions — not to slow things down, but because she needs the "customer truth" before she can write anything.
- Common objections: "How do we explain this benefit to a 14-year-old?" / "What's the one-sentence hook?" / "Who are we talking to with this feature?"
- What she needs to say yes: A clear user benefit that she can turn into a message, enough lead time to prepare launch assets (minimum 2 weeks), and clarity on which persona this feature primarily serves.

---

## Ron Mizrahi — Engineering Lead

- Optimizes for: Clean architecture, realistic scoping, and no surprise pivots mid-sprint. Cares deeply about technical quality and doesn't like shipping things he knows will come back as debt.
- Biggest fear: Being asked to build something under deadline pressure that hasn't been properly thought through — ends up with a mess that's expensive to fix.
- Communication style: Direct, technically precise, and skeptical of vague requirements. Respects tradeoff discussions — if you acknowledge the tradeoff, he'll engage. If you pretend there isn't one, he'll find it and raise it at the worst time.
- Common objections: "This is more complex than it looks." / "We'll need to refactor the video pipeline before we can do this cleanly." / "Can we scope this down and still get the signal we need?"
- What he needs to say yes: A clear and complete spec with edge cases considered, priorities ranked (what's the MVP vs. the nice-to-have), scope boundaries defined, and explicit acknowledgement of the tradeoffs the team is accepting.
