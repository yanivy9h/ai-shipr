# INT-003 — Parent Interview (Mira-type, active subscriber)

## Participant
- Name: Rachel Biton
- Age: 44
- Profile: Working mother, two kids. Her son Idan (14) plays basketball. Pays ₪29/month for CourtIQ. 3 months into the subscription, has renewed twice without cancelling. Does not use the app herself.
- Recruited: Via in-app prompt shown to parents (on the "parent view" onboarding email that was sent at signup)
- Interviewed by: Dana Avraham
- Date: 2026-02-20
- Duration: 31 minutes (Zoom, during her lunch break)

---

## Key Quotes

> "I have no idea what's in the app. I never opened it. I just know Idan is going outside more, so something is working."

> "He asked me to keep the subscription. That was my sign. If he's asking me not to cancel it, it means he's actually using it."

> "I don't need to see every drill. I just need one number. One thing that tells me — this month was better than last month."

> "The renewal email comes and I think about it for a second, then I pay. But I'm basically going on faith. I have no data."

> "If I knew he completed 40 drills this month versus 12 last month — that's it. I'd never cancel."

---

## What We Heard

**On her decision to keep subscribing:**
Rachel renewed based on behavioural signals she observed at home — Idan going outside more, mentioning drills to his dad. She has zero in-app visibility. Her renewal was driven entirely by Idan's enthusiasm, which she knew could fade at any time. She described her trust in the product as "fragile but holding."

**On what she needs to feel confident:**
She was clear and consistent: one number. Not a detailed breakdown. Not graphs. Just a simple comparison — this month vs. last month. She said it multiple times in different ways. The format she described unprompted sounded like a single-metric weekly email: "8 drills completed this week" with a month-over-month comparison.

**On the parent dashboard:**
She was unaware one existed (it doesn't yet). When told it was being built, she said: "That's exactly what I need. But send it to my email — I'm not going to open an app for this."

**On premium vs. free:**
"If the parent thing costs extra I'd pay for it. It's the thing I actually care about. The drills are for him, the dashboard is for me."

---

## What We Learned

1. **Email is the only channel that works for Mira-type parents.** She will not open a parent app. The value delivery mechanism must be a weekly email, not an in-app screen.
2. **One number is enough.** She doesn't want a dashboard — she wants a single comparable metric. "This month vs. last month" is the format. This is significantly simpler than what the team has been designing.
3. **Premium pricing for the parent layer has signal.** Rachel volunteered that she'd pay extra for the parent report. This is meaningful — it suggests the parent dashboard could be a premium-only feature without churn risk for engaged parents like her.

## Insight Flag
Rachel's "send it to my email" comment challenges the assumption that a parent dashboard (in-app) is the right delivery mechanism. An automated weekly email digest may be faster to build and higher-impact than a dedicated web view. Worth testing email-first before building the full parent screen.
