# INT-004 — Parent Interview (Mira-type, churned)

## Participant
- Name: Sigal Hamami
- Age: 39
- Profile: Working parent, two kids. Her son Yoav (12) plays in a recreational league. Subscribed to CourtIQ for 2 months, cancelled at the start of month 3. Reason given in cancellation survey: "My son stopped using it."
- Recruited: Via post-cancellation email survey; Sigal agreed to a follow-up call
- Interviewed by: Dana Avraham
- Date: 2026-02-22
- Duration: 19 minutes (phone call — shorter than planned, she had a meeting)

---

## Key Quotes

> "He used it for maybe two weeks. Then it just... stopped. I found out when I saw the charge on my card and thought — wait, is he even using this?"

> "I asked him and he said 'sometimes.' That's not a yes."

> "I wasn't going to keep paying ₪29 a month for 'sometimes.'"

> "I didn't get any email from the app. Nothing. For two months. No summary, no 'Yoav did 3 drills this week,' nothing. I had no idea."

> "If they had sent me something — anything — showing me he was using it, I probably would have kept it. I just had nothing to go on."

> *(when asked if she'd come back)* "If I knew it was actually being used, yes. The content is probably fine. I just couldn't see anything."

---

## What We Heard

**On the churn moment:**
The credit card charge was the trigger. Without any usage signal from the app, Sigal had no reason to believe the subscription was working. The app never reached out to her — not once in two months. She made the cancellation decision in the moment, with zero data.

**On her son's usage drop-off:**
She believed Yoav used the app for about two weeks before the habit broke. She doesn't know why it stopped — he gave vague answers. But the core issue for her wasn't why he stopped; it was that she had no way to know if he ever started in a meaningful way.

**On what would have prevented the churn:**
She was direct: a single weekly email. "Even just 'Yoav completed 2 drills this week.' That's it. I would have seen it, thought okay he's doing something, and kept paying." She explicitly said she didn't need a detailed report — frequency of contact mattered more than depth.

**On product awareness:**
She had no idea there was supposed to be a parent view. She only saw the player-facing app when her son set it up. The product never communicated the parent value proposition after the initial signup.

---

## What We Learned

1. **The churn trigger is a silent app, not a bad product.** Sigal didn't cancel because CourtIQ was bad. She cancelled because it was invisible. One email per week would have changed the outcome.
2. **The parent communication gap is a retention leak, not a feature gap.** This could be addressed without building a parent dashboard at all — a simple automated weekly email with drill count + streak data would be sufficient for this persona.
3. **Post-signup parent nurturing is broken.** The product communicates well at signup (onboarding email) and at renewal (charge notification) but not in between. The middle 30 days are silent — which is exactly when parents are deciding whether it's worth keeping.

## Insight Flag
INT-003 (Rachel, active) and INT-004 (Sigal, churned) are telling the same story from opposite outcomes. The only difference: Rachel has indirect signals from Yoav's behaviour at home. Sigal had nothing. The product has no role in Sigal's case. That's a distribution problem: we're building value for the player but failing to signal that value to the payer.
