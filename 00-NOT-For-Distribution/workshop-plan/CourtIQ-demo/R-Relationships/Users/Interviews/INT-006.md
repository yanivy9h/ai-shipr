# INT-006 — Coach Interview (Coach David-type, enthusiast)

## Participant
- Name: Gabi Levi
- Age: 35
- Profile: Youth basketball coach for 6 years. Coaches a competitive club team of 14 players, ages 12–15, in a regional league. Works in tech (developer) — comfortable with apps, uses fitness and coaching tools personally. Heard about CourtIQ from a parent of one of his players.
- Recruited: Inbound — emailed support asking when a coach dashboard would be available
- Interviewed by: Dana Avraham
- Date: 2026-02-26
- Duration: 41 minutes (Zoom — Gabi was enthusiastic and hard to cut off)

---

## Key Quotes

> "The problem isn't that kids don't want to practice. It's that they don't know HOW to practice alone. They need structure. That's what CourtIQ gives them."

> "I want to assign three drills before Tuesday's session and see on Tuesday morning who did them. That's the whole thing. That's all I need."

> "Right now I send a WhatsApp message and I get back heart emojis. Heart emojis don't tell me if anyone actually drilled."

> "If I can see that 9 out of 14 players completed the dribbling drill, I know who to pair in the warm-up. I know who to push. I know where to start the session."

> "I already recommend CourtIQ to parents. I have three players on it right now — they subscribed because I mentioned it. Imagine if I could assign them drills directly."

> "Build this right and coaches become your sales team. We talk to each other at every tournament. If one coach swears by it, five coaches sign up."

> *(on payment)* "I wouldn't expect to pay. But if the platform is good enough, I'd put it in front of the club and they'd cover it."

## What We Heard

**On the current gap:**
Gabi has already internalised the CourtIQ value proposition better than most users. He sees the product clearly: structured practice + completion tracking = accountability layer between sessions. His frustration is that the coach role doesn't exist yet — he can recommend it, but he can't assign, track, or close the loop.

**On what he'd do with the assignment feature:**
He described his use case in detail, unprompted: assign 3 specific drills on Sunday night, check completion on Tuesday morning at 7am before leaving for the gym, adjust session plan based on who prepared. He already knows what drills he'd assign for each week's focus area.

**On network effects:**
Gabi volunteered the coach-as-distribution insight without prompting. He's already sent 3 players to CourtIQ through word of mouth. If he could assign drills officially, his recommendation would carry institutional weight — "my coach assigned it" is much stronger than "my coach mentioned an app."

**On monetisation:**
He doesn't expect to pay personally but flagged that the club might fund it if positioned as a club tool. This opens a B2B pricing angle that hasn't been explored — club licenses rather than individual coach accounts.

**On product quality:**
He had thoughtful feedback on the drill content: some drills lack a "how to know if you're doing it right" component. He wants brief success criteria in each drill — "if you can do this at speed without breaking form, you've got it."

---

## What We Learned

1. **Gabi is the ideal beta coach for INI-003.** He already distributes the product, understands the use case precisely, and has a ready roster. He should be in the first 5 coaches for the invite-only beta.
2. **"Coaches become your sales team" is the growth thesis made concrete.** He's already doing it unpaid and without tools. The assignment feature is the unlock that makes this scalable and trackable.
3. **Club licensing is an untested monetisation angle.** Gabi's comment about the club covering costs suggests there may be a B2B motion here — not just coach-individual accounts. Worth flagging for the INI-003 post-beta evaluation.
4. **Success criteria in drills is a product quality gap.** Gabi and Tal (INT-002) both raised the issue of not knowing if you're doing a drill correctly. This is a content gap, not a feature gap — could be addressed by adding a "you've got it when..." line to each drill card.

## Insight Flag
Gabi's profile breaks the assumption that coaches need heavy convincing. There is a segment of tech-forward, younger coaches who already see the value — the product just isn't meeting them with the tools they need. The invite-only beta should target this segment first, not the sceptical Eran-type. Validate with the enthusiast cohort before broadening.
