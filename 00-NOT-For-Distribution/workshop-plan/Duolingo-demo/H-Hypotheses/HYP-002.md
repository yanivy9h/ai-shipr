# HYP — Sending push notifications based on each user's historical usage time increases DAU by 15%

## Related Initiative
- [[Initiative: Smart notification scheduler]] — INI-002

## Risk Level
High

## Why this might fail
- Historical usage time is noisy — users don't always open the app at the same time every day. The signal may not be strong enough for meaningful personalization.
- Even a well-timed notification is still a notification. If the content is generic, timing alone won't overcome notification fatigue.
- The ML model may not be accurate enough in the first version — a poorly timed "personalized" notification could feel worse than a consistent one.

## Test Plan
- Method: A/B test — 50% of users receive notifications at their historically peak usage time (model output), 50% receive notifications at the current default time (8pm local)
- Who/where: Users who have enabled notifications and have at least 14 days of session history. Global.
- Duration: 28 days (longer window needed to account for day-of-week variation)
- Dependency: Requires data science team model — available Q3

## Metric + Threshold
- Metric: DAU in the test group vs. control group, measured as 7-day rolling average
- Success threshold: +15% DAU in the personalized group vs. control
- Failure threshold: Less than +6% — below this, personalized timing is not the lever and we investigate content instead
