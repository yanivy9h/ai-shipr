# HYP — Users who connect with at least one friend in their first week have 2x higher D30 retention

## Related Initiative
- [[Initiative: Friends & social layer MVP]] — INI-003

## Risk Level
Low (internal data already supports this directionally — now testing whether we can actively drive it)

## Why this might fail
- Correlation, not causation: users who connect with friends may already be more motivated — the social feature isn't creating retention, it's attracting already-retained users
- Friend-finding is high friction — if finding people you know on Duolingo is hard, most users won't bother and the experiment will understate the true effect
- Social pressure could backfire for some users — seeing a friend with a 200-day streak when you have 3 might cause discouragement, not motivation

## Test Plan
- Method: Prompted vs. not prompted — 50% of new users see a "Find friends on Duolingo" prompt on day 2, 50% do not (friends feature still accessible but not prompted)
- Who/where: New users globally, iOS and Android
- Duration: 30 days (measuring D30 retention)

## Metric + Threshold
- Metric: D30 retention rate (test group vs. control group)
- Success threshold: +8 percentage points in D30 retention for prompted users who connect with at least one friend
- Failure threshold: Less than +3 percentage points — social prompt alone is not driving retention
