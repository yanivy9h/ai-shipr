# HYP — Reducing the daily XP goal from 20 to 10 for lapsed users increases 7-day re-engagement rate by 25%

## Related Initiative
- Standalone experiment — not yet linked to a formal initiative

## Risk Level
Low

## Why this might fail
- Lapsed users may have stopped for reasons unrelated to the daily goal — relationship ended, course finished, life got busy. A lower goal won't bring them back.
- Users who return with a lower goal may complete less content and feel less satisfaction — the bar was too low to feel meaningful
- This could dilute DAU quality — users return once to "check in" and leave again, inflating re-engagement numbers without improving real retention

## Test Plan
- Method: A/B test — lapsed users (no session in 14+ days) receive a re-engagement push notification. Test group: notification says "Just 2 minutes today — a 10 XP goal is waiting for you." Control group: standard re-engagement notification.
- Who/where: Users lapsed 14–60 days, global
- Duration: 14 days

## Metric + Threshold
- Metric: 7-day re-engagement rate (% of lapsed users who complete at least one lesson in the 7 days after receiving the notification)
- Success threshold: +25% vs. control
- Failure threshold: Less than +10% — lapsed users are not responding to goal reduction and we investigate other re-engagement angles
