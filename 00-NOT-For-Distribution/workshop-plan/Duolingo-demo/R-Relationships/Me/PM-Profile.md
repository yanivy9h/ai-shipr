# PM Profile

## My role in the team
- Responsibilities: Own the Habits & Retention roadmap — streak mechanics, push notifications, re-engagement, and social features. Run discovery, write experiment briefs, align engineering and stakeholders on priorities.
- Decision ownership: Experiment design, feature scope, prioritisation tradeoffs. Escalate to VP Product on anything that touches monetization or company-level notification policy.
- Interfaces with: Noa (Eng Lead) daily in sprint, Data Science team on experiment design, Trust & Safety on notification changes, PMM (Shir) on launch messaging, Finance (Roi) on any pricing decisions

## My biggest challenge right now
Notification opt-out is at 23% and rising. Every opted-out user is a user I can no longer reach — and I have no fast solution. The right fix (personalized notification timing) requires an ML model that won't be ready until Q3. Meanwhile, I have two experiments running and a third decision blocked because Finance hasn't signed off on the Streak Repair price point. I need to move three things forward in parallel without losing focus.

## Where I spend most of my time
- Experiment design and analysis: writing briefs, reviewing data, figuring out what the signal is actually telling us
- Stakeholder alignment: Trust & Safety, Finance, and the wellness advisory group all have veto power on things I want to ship. Getting sign-off is slower than building.
- User interviews: every two weeks I talk to churned users — people who had streaks, broke them, and left. That's where the real insight is.

## Where I want leverage from AI-SHIPR
- Faster hypothesis framing: I arrive at my thinking slowly. I want to say "here's the problem" and get back a structured set of assumptions to pressure-test.
- Better stakeholder communication: Finance and Trust & Safety respond to data and risk framing, not product enthusiasm. I need first drafts that speak their language.
- Decision clarity: I have three open decisions this week and I keep avoiding one of them. I need a forcing function.

## My operating preference
Data first — I don't ship on instinct. If I can't define the metric and the threshold before we build, I'm not confident enough to commit the sprint.
