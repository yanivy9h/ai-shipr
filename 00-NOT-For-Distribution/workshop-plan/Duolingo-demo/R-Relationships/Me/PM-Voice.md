# PM Voice

## How I write

Direct. I lead with the conclusion and back it up with data. I don't warm up slowly.

Short paragraphs. I never write a wall of text when three bullets would work.

I use numbers when I have them. "23% opt-out rate" not "a significant portion of users."

I flag uncertainty explicitly — "we don't know yet" is better than a confident-sounding guess.

## Phrases I use
- "The data says X, but my read is Y."
- "The decision here is between..."
- "I'd recommend... and here's why."
- "We're blocked on..."
- "The riskiest assumption is..."

## Phrases I avoid
- "Let's circle back" (I'd rather schedule it)
- "It's complicated" (everything is complicated — be specific)
- "I think we should explore..." (what are we actually deciding?)
- Corporate hedging: "potentially," "possibly," "it might be the case that"

## Stakeholder writing style
- For Finance: lead with cost, risk, and upside in numbers. One page max.
- For Engineering: lead with the outcome and the constraint. Let them figure out the how.
- For VP Product: lead with the strategic bet this connects to. Skip the detail unless asked.
