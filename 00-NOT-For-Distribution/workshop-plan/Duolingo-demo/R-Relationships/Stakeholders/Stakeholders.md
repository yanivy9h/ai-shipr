# Stakeholders

## Noa Katz — Engineering Lead

- Role: Leads the 4-person Habits & Retention engineering team
- What she cares about: Scope clarity before sprint starts. She doesn't want to discover requirements mid-sprint. She asks "how do we know this is done?" before agreeing to anything.
- How to communicate: Written spec first, then sync. She reads everything before a meeting and expects you to have too.
- Current tension: One senior engineer (Lior) is partially allocated to the platform migration. She's protective of the team's capacity and will push back on anything that looks underscoped.
- Decision power: Feasibility and effort estimates. Can block a sprint item by flagging it as underspecified.

## Roi Ben-David — Finance

- Role: Finance lead for the consumer product line
- What he cares about: Revenue impact and risk. Any pricing change — even a $0.99 experiment — requires his sign-off. He moves slowly and needs numbers.
- How to communicate: One-page brief with numbers. Cost, expected revenue impact, risk of downside. No product enthusiasm — he doesn't care about the feature, he cares about the P&L.
- Current tension: Has not signed off on the Streak Repair price point for free users. This is blocking EXP-001. The bottleneck is getting 30 minutes on his calendar.
- Decision power: Hard veto on anything involving pricing or payment flows for free users.

## Shir Levi — PMM (Product Marketing Manager)

- Role: Owns in-app messaging, notification copy, and launch communications for Habits & Retention
- What she cares about: Voice and tone. She'll rewrite anything that sounds off-brand. She's protective of Duo (the owl) and how he's used in messaging.
- How to communicate: Share copy early — she hates being asked to review something the day before launch. Involve her in the experiment brief, not just the launch.
- Current tension: The "sad owl" notification copy that drove Shira (INT-001) to turn off notifications was Shir's work. She doesn't know we're hearing this in interviews. Handle carefully.
- Decision power: Controls notification copy and in-app messaging. Can delay a launch by flagging brand risk.

## Trust & Safety (team, not an individual)

- Role: Review and approve any changes to notification frequency, content, or targeting
- What they care about: User wellbeing and regulatory compliance. They have a 2-week review SLA.
- How to communicate: Written request with specific change description, affected audience size, and risk assessment. They don't take verbal requests.
- Current tension: Notification timing personalization (INI-002) will require their review. Building this into the Q3 timeline.
- Decision power: Can block any notification experiment. Non-negotiable.

## Wellness Advisory Group (external)

- Role: Reviews social features for potential negative psychological impact
- What they care about: Comparison mechanics, shame triggers, addictive patterns
- How to communicate: Design mocks + written rationale. They need to see what the feature looks like, not just hear a description.
- Current tension: INI-003 (social layer) requires their review before shipping. EXP-002 (friends prompt) was already approved — but leaderboard feature will need a separate review.
- Decision power: Can block any social feature. Rare but real.
