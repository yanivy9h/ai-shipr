# INT-001 — Churned user interview (Tamar-type)

## Participant
- Name: Shira Bar-On
- Age: 29
- Profile: Teacher. Started Duolingo to learn Italian. Had a 33-day streak. Churned 4 months ago. Has not returned.
- Recruited: Re-engagement email campaign — clicked the email, agreed to an interview
- Interviewed by: Dana Klein
- Date: 2026-02-10
- Duration: 31 minutes (Zoom)

---

## Key Quotes

> "I missed one day. Just one. And when I opened the app the next morning it told me my streak was gone. Like, thanks. That's really helpful."

> "I turned off the notifications because they kept calling me sad. The owl was sad. I'm a grown adult. I don't need a cartoon owl guilt-tripping me."

> "I would have paid to keep my streak. Actually. I would have paid. Nobody offered me that."

> "I still have it on my phone. I open it sometimes and then close it. I don't know why I don't just do a lesson. Something about starting again feels heavy."

---

## What We Heard

**On the streak break:**
Shira described the moment she saw "0" as disproportionately demoralizing. She had a bad day at school, forgot to open the app, and the next morning the streak was gone. She felt punished for a bad day, not supported through one.

**On notifications:**
Three things bothered her: the frequency (she said it felt like "every few hours" — may be an exaggeration but the feeling was real), the tone ("sad owl" imagery made her feel blamed), and the timing (notifications arrived mid-morning while she was teaching — impossible to respond to and annoying to dismiss).

**On Streak Repair:**
She didn't know it existed. When told it was available for subscribers, she said she would have paid $2–3 to restore her streak immediately. "Why didn't anyone offer me that?"

**On coming back:**
She wants to but the accumulated gap feels like a wall. "It's been four months. Where do I even start?" She mentioned she'd need something to feel like a reset, not a return to failure.

---

## Insights

1. **Streak break is the moment of maximum churn risk — and maximum intervention opportunity.** Shira would have paid immediately. The offer needs to be there at that exact moment, not buried in settings.
2. **Notification tone is actively creating negative brand association.** "Sad owl" is remembered four months later. This is not a minor UX detail.
3. **Re-engagement requires a reframe, not a reminder.** She doesn't need to be told she's been away. She needs to be told it's easy to come back. Language matters.
