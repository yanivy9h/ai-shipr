# INT-002 — Active power user interview (Eran-type)

## Participant
- Name: Avi Stern
- Age: 41
- Profile: Product manager at a fintech company. Learning Mandarin. 187-day streak. Uses Duolingo every morning before work.
- Recruited: In-app survey — opted in to user research
- Interviewed by: Dana Klein
- Date: 2026-02-14
- Duration: 26 minutes (Zoom)

---

## Key Quotes

> "The streak is the only thing keeping me honest. If I didn't have the streak, I would have stopped after week two."

> "I bought four Streak Freezes before my trip to London last year. I was more stressed about the streak than about the presentation I was flying to give."

> "My wife thinks it's funny that I care this much about a language app. But I've learned more Mandarin in six months than in three years of trying other things."

> "I'd pay more for Duolingo if they gave me better data about my progress. Right now I just see my streak. I want to see if I'm actually getting better."

---

## What We Heard

**On the streak as a mechanic:**
Avi credits the streak entirely for his consistency. He's tried language apps before — none stuck. The streak created an external commitment he couldn't negotiate with. He's explicit about this: the streak isn't about learning, it's about showing up.

**On streak anxiety:**
He's aware the anxiety is disproportionate ("more stressed about the streak than my presentation"). He doesn't see this as a problem — he sees it as the feature working. But he acknowledged that if he ever lost a long streak he'd probably stop.

**On Streak Freeze:**
He uses Streak Freeze as insurance. He's bought it multiple times. He doesn't think of it as a workaround — he thinks of it as responsible planning.

**On progress visibility:**
His one frustration: the streak tells him he showed up, but not whether he's improving. He wants a vocabulary count, a complexity score, something. "I've done 187 days. I have no idea if I'm at A2 or B1."

---

## Insights

1. **For power users, streak anxiety is a feature.** They are self-aware about it and they accept the tradeoff. Don't remove the pressure — these users are opting in to it.
2. **Streak Freeze is already solving the travel problem for aware users.** The gap is users who don't know it exists or don't plan ahead.
3. **Progress beyond streaks is an unmet need for power users.** They've maxed out what the current product offers — streak data is not enough. This is a retention risk at 6–12 months.
