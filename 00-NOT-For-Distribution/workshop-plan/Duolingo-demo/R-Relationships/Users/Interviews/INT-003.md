# INT-003 — Casual user interview (Maya-type)

## Participant
- Name: Lior Ben-Tzvi
- Age: 34
- Profile: Graphic designer. Learning Portuguese for a Brazil trip planned for August. 12-day streak. Has restarted twice.
- Recruited: In-app prompt after 14 days of usage
- Interviewed by: Dana Klein
- Date: 2026-02-19
- Duration: 22 minutes (Zoom)

---

## Key Quotes

> "I do it on the train in the morning. It's perfect for that — five minutes, done. But weekends I forget completely."

> "I know I've broken my streak twice already. I just start again. It's fine, I'm not that attached to it."

> "I use it because it's fun, not because I have to. The moment it stops being fun I'll just stop."

> "I don't need to be fluent. I need to order food, ask for the bathroom, and maybe understand someone if they talk slowly."

---

## What We Heard

**On the streak:**
Lior has a fundamentally different relationship with the streak than Eran-type users. She breaks it, shrugs, restarts. It's not emotionally charged. This is interesting — she's not churning despite the streak breaks. But she's also not deeply engaged.

**On the routine:**
The commute is her trigger. On weekdays, opening Duolingo is almost automatic. On weekends, there's no trigger, so it doesn't happen. She's not making a choice not to use it — it just doesn't occur to her.

**On her goal:**
Her goal is narrow and specific: function in Brazil in August. She's not thinking about language learning as a lifelong habit. Once the trip happens, she'll probably stop.

**On notifications:**
She ignores them. She doesn't find them annoying enough to turn off, but she never acts on them. "I do it when I do it. A notification doesn't change that."

---

## Insights

1. **For goal-bound users, the product needs to connect to the goal, not the streak.** A "Brazil countdown" or "lessons before your trip" frame would be more motivating than streak continuity.
2. **Routine-dependent users won't be solved by notifications.** Lior doesn't need a reminder — she needs a weekend trigger. Notifications at the wrong time (for her, that's any non-commute moment) are noise.
3. **Streak indifference is not a problem to fix — it's a signal about this user's relationship with the product.** She'll churn after August regardless. The question is whether we can convert her to a post-trip continuous learner.
