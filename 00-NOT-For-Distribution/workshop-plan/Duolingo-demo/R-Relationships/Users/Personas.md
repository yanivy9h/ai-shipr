# Personas

## Persona 1 — Maya "The Commuter" Levy

- Role / profile: 31-year-old marketing manager in Tel Aviv. Learning Spanish for a trip to Barcelona in June. Uses Duolingo on her morning train commute — 20 minutes each way.
- Job-to-be-done: Learn enough Spanish to get through a restaurant, ask for directions, and not embarrass herself. She doesn't need fluency — she needs confidence for one trip.
- Biggest pain: She breaks her streak every weekend because she forgets to open the app when her commute routine disappears. Monday comes and she sees "0 day streak" and feels like she failed. She's restarted three times already.
- Emotional drivers: Wants to feel prepared and capable. Mild embarrassment about not speaking the language of a country she's visiting. The streak breaking feels like personal failure more than a product problem.
- What success looks like: She gets to Barcelona, orders from a menu in Spanish without pointing, and tells her friends "Duolingo actually worked." She never had to think about the streak — it just kept going.
- Where they show up: Mobile only. Train, 7:30–8am and 6:30–7pm on weekdays. Almost no usage on weekends. Short sessions, 5–7 minutes.

## Persona 2 — Eran "The Streak Keeper" Mizrahi

- Role / profile: 44-year-old software architect. Learning Japanese — has been for 14 months. Current streak: 218 days. Duolingo is the first thing he does after his morning coffee.
- Job-to-be-done: Maintain a consistent daily learning habit. The language itself almost doesn't matter anymore — the streak is the thing. He's proud of 218 days and terrified of losing it.
- Biggest pain: He's traveling to Japan next month for a conference. He's terrified of time zones and losing his streak while on a long flight. He's already bought three Streak Freezes. The anxiety is disproportionate to the actual stakes — and he knows it.
- Emotional drivers: Discipline and identity. He's the person who "does Duolingo every day." He tells people about the streak. Losing it would feel like losing part of how he sees himself.
- What success looks like: He lands in Tokyo, opens the app, and his streak is intact. He completes a lesson before his first meeting. 219 days.
- Where they show up: Mobile, every morning, same time. Very consistent. Also reviews his progress stats regularly — he's a data person.

## Persona 3 — Tamar "The Ghost" Cohen

- Role / profile: 27-year-old nurse. Started Duolingo 8 months ago to learn French — had a goal of moving to Paris "someday." Got to a 41-day streak. Then had a rough week at work, missed two days, and never came back.
- Job-to-be-done: Nothing right now. She's not using the app. The app still sends her notifications. She turned them off after the third one called her "sad" for not practicing.
- Biggest pain: The moment she broke her streak, the app made her feel bad about it. She felt judged by a language app. She uninstalled it for two weeks, reinstalled it "to try again," and never opened it. It lives on her phone as a monument to a goal she gave up on.
- Emotional drivers: Shame. She doesn't want to "fail again." The app reminds her of a goal that feels out of reach now. She'd come back if she felt like she could start without the pressure of the streak history following her.
- What success looks like: She re-engages without feeling like she's starting over from failure. Maybe the app reframes it as "picking up where she left off" rather than showing her a 0-day streak. She does one lesson. Then another the next day.
- Where they show up: Not showing up right now. If she re-engages, it would be mobile, evenings, short sessions.
