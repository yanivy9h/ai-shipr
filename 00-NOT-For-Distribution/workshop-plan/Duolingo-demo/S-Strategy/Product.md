# Product

## What we're building

Duolingo is a language learning app available on iOS, Android, and web. The core loop: open the app, complete a lesson (3–7 minutes), earn XP, maintain your streak. Miss a day — lose your streak. The streak is the product's backbone.

## The team — Habits & Retention (this PM's scope)

This team owns the mechanics that bring users back every day:
- Streak system (streak display, streak repair, streak freeze)
- Push notifications (timing, content, frequency, opt-out handling)
- Re-engagement flows (lapsed user campaigns, win-back)
- Social features (friends, leaderboards, accountability)

We do not own: lesson content, language curricula, monetization, or the Super Duolingo subscription (though our retention metrics directly affect conversion).

## Current state

- 40M+ DAU globally
- Average session length: 6 minutes
- D7 retention (new users): 38%
- Streak continuation rate (users who had a 7+ day streak): 61% — meaning 39% of users with an established streak break it within the next 30 days
- Notification opt-out rate: 23% — up from 16% two years ago. This is a red flag.

## The core tension

The streak is the most powerful retention mechanic in the app. It also creates the most anxiety. Users who break a streak often feel shame and stop returning — exactly the opposite of what we want. We are over-relying on a mechanic that is working for some users and actively pushing others away.
