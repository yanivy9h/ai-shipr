# Strategic Bets (max 3)

Documents the 1–3 areas where the team is placing its bets this period.
The most-read file in the system — sprint planning, initiative review, OKRs, and business cases all reference it.
Update quarterly or when a bet is confirmed or killed.

---

## Bet 1 — Streak Repair as a retention rescue, not a monetization tool
- Why this matters: 39% of users who establish a 7+ day streak break it within 30 days. When they do, the majority never return. We have a repair mechanic (pay to restore your streak) but it's currently only for Super Duolingo subscribers and positioned as a premium perk. Our hypothesis is that offering Streak Repair to all users at a low-friction price point — or even free with a daily limit — would rescue a meaningful portion of the churning 39%.
- Who it serves: Maya (casual learner who broke her streak on a travel day and felt stupid), Eran (streak-obsessed user who would pay anything to not lose 200 days)
- What success looks like: Streak continuation rate improves from 61% to 72%. Same-day churn after a streak break drops by 30%.
- Time horizon: Q2 2026

## Bet 2 — Smarter notifications that users don't turn off
- Why this matters: Notification opt-out is at 23% and rising. We are training users to mute us. The current system sends reminders at fixed times regardless of when the user actually learns. We know from session data when each user typically opens the app — we are not using that signal. A notification at the right moment is a helpful nudge. A notification at the wrong moment is spam.
- Who it serves: All DAU — but especially Tamar (lapsed user who turned off notifications after receiving three reminders in a row while in a meeting)
- What success looks like: Notification opt-out rate drops from 23% to below 17%. DAU increases as more users receive timely nudges rather than disabling them.
- Time horizon: Q3 2026

## Bet 3 — Social accountability as the second retention layer
- Why this matters: The streak works for solo motivation, but it is a single point of failure. Once it breaks, there is nothing holding a user in the app. Social features — seeing a friend's streak, being in a weekly leaderboard, receiving a nudge from a friend — create a second reason to show up. Users with at least one friend on Duolingo have 2.1x higher D30 retention (internal data, 2025 cohort study). We are not building for this.
- Who it serves: Casual learners who need external accountability more than internal discipline
- What success looks like: D7 retention improves by 6+ percentage points for users who connect with at least one friend in their first week.
- Time horizon: Q4 2026
