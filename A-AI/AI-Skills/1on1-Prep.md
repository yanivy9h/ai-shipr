# Skill: 1on1 Prep

## Purpose
Structure 1:1 agendas from active initiatives, pending decisions, and blockers.
Turn a recurring calendar event into a high-leverage conversation.
Make sure the PM walks in with a point of view — not just a status update.

A 1:1 without preparation is a check-in.
A 1:1 with this skill is a decision session.

## When to Use
- The morning before a 1:1 with your manager, skip-level, or key stakeholder
- When you have multiple open threads and need to prioritize what to raise
- When you need to escalate something and want to frame it correctly
- When a 1:1 has been unproductive — use this to reset the format

## Behavior Rules
- Read active initiatives and pending decisions before generating the agenda
- Prioritize decisions that are blocking progress — these must be raised, not deferred
- Separate: what you need from them vs what you are informing them of
- Do not fill the 1:1 with status — they can read status. Use the time for what requires them.
- Every agenda item must have a clear outcome: decision / alignment / unblock / inform
- If there is an escalation, frame it with context, your recommendation, and the specific ask
- Flag topics that are time-sensitive — these move to the top regardless of preference

## Input Expectations
Provide:
- Who the 1:1 is with (manager / skip-level / peer / stakeholder)
- Available time (30 min / 60 min)
- Active initiatives you want to discuss

Optional:
- Pending decisions from Decision-Architect or initiative files
- Any blockers or fires that need escalation
- Topics you want to raise but are unsure how to frame

## Output Structure

### 1:1 Prep: [PM Name] ↔ [Other Person]

**Date:** [Date]
**Duration:** [N minutes]
**With:** [Role — not just name]

---

#### Pre-Meeting Read (30 seconds)

> What I need from this conversation:
> [One sentence — the single most important outcome from this 1:1]

---

#### Agenda

**Time allocation:** [N minutes per section — must sum to available time]

---

**1. Decisions Required — [N min]**
*Things that are blocking progress and require their input or authority*

| Decision | Context (1 line) | My Recommendation | What I need from them |
|----------|-----------------|-------------------|-----------------------|
| [Decision] | [Why it matters now] | [Your POV] | Approval / Input / Alignment |

---

**2. Escalations — [N min]**
*Issues that have exceeded your authority or timeline to resolve alone*

| Issue | What I've tried | Why I need them | Specific ask |
|-------|----------------|-----------------|-------------|
| [Issue] | [Actions taken] | [Why this escalation is necessary] | [Exact ask — not "help me"] |

---

**3. Initiative Updates — [N min]**
*Status on what they care about — brief, signal-focused, not comprehensive*

| Initiative | Stage | Green / Yellow / Red | One thing they should know |
|-----------|-------|---------------------|---------------------------|
| [Name] | [Stage] | 🟢 / 🟡 / 🔴 | [The signal — not the full story] |

---

**4. Their Agenda — [N min]**
*Reserve time for what they want to discuss — do not fill the full hour*

> Placeholder: "What's on your end?"
> Do not skip this. The best 1:1s go both directions.

---

#### Escalation Frame (if applicable)

Use this structure when raising a difficult topic:

> **Situation:** [What is happening — factual, no spin]
> **Impact:** [What it is affecting — metric, initiative, team]
> **What I've tried:** [Actions already taken]
> **My recommendation:** [Your POV — own it]
> **What I need:** [Specific ask — decision / resource / air cover]

---

#### After the 1:1

- [ ] Log decisions made into the relevant initiative file
- [ ] Update initiative Stage if something unblocked
- [ ] Note anything escalated to Learning.md if it reveals a pattern
- [ ] Set follow-up if any item was deferred
