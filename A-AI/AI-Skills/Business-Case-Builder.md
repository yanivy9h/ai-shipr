# Skill: Business Case Builder

## Purpose
Structure a funding pitch for leadership.
Convert a product opportunity into a business case with explicit problem framing,
quantified impact, cost estimate, and a clear ask.

This is The Pitch. Where product teams live or die.

## When to Use
- Quarterly or annual planning — when competing for roadmap allocation
- When leadership requests justification for a proposed initiative
- Before presenting a new strategic bet to exec or stakeholders
- When a team is being asked to choose between two investment directions
- Any time you need to answer: "Why should we fund this over everything else?"

## Behavior Rules
- Require a specific business problem — "improve user experience" is not a business problem
- Force impact quantification — estimate revenue, cost reduction, risk mitigation, or strategic positioning. Ranges are acceptable. "Unknown" is not.
- Require a cost estimate — time, headcount, or opportunity cost. Vague cost = vague ask.
- Link explicitly to a Strategic Bet — a business case with no strategic anchor will not survive exec review
- Surface the cost of inaction — what happens if we do not fund this
- Anticipate the hardest objection leadership will raise — address it in the case, not in Q&A
- Flag assumptions that are load-bearing — if one assumption is wrong, the case changes significantly
- Output must be usable as input for Pitch-Deck-Builder

## Input Expectations
Provide:
- The business problem you are trying to solve (plain language)
- Your proposed solution or initiative direction
- Any data you have on the problem (size, frequency, cost)

Optional:
- The Strategic Bet this links to
- Known objections from leadership
- Competitive or market context

## Output Structure

### Business Case: [Initiative Name]

#### The Problem
- **Business problem:** [Specific — what is broken, expensive, or missing in the business]
- **Who it affects:** [User segment / Internal team / Market position]
- **Scale:** [How many users, how often, what it costs today — quantified where possible]
- **Cost of inaction:** [What gets worse if we do nothing — in 6 months, in 12 months]

#### The Proposed Solution
- **What we will build:** [1-2 sentences — outcome, not feature list]
- **Why this approach:** [Why this solution over alternatives — 2 bullets max]
- **Strategic link:** [Bet # and why this bet now]

#### Impact Estimate
| Metric | Current | Projected | Confidence | Basis |
|--------|---------|-----------|------------|-------|
| [Revenue / Retention / Cost / Risk / Other] | ... | ... | High / Med / Low | [Data / Analogy / Assumption] |

- **Primary impact:** [The one number that justifies this investment]
- **Secondary effects:** [2 bullets max]

#### Cost Estimate
- **Engineering:** [Weeks / sprints / headcount]
- **Design:** [If applicable]
- **Other:** [Infrastructure, external, opportunity cost]
- **Total estimated investment:** [Range acceptable]

#### Key Assumptions
| Assumption | Risk if wrong | Testable before full build? |
|-----------|--------------|----------------------------|
| ... | High / Med / Low | Yes / No |

#### The Ask
- **What we need:** [Headcount / Budget / Prioritization / Executive sponsorship]
- **Decision needed by:** [Date]
- **What happens if delayed:** [Specific consequence]

#### Hardest Objection — Pre-addressed
> "**[Most likely leadership objection]**"
> Response: [Direct answer — not defensive, not dismissive]
