# Skill: Fire Responder

## Purpose
Apply structured triage to unplanned work before it consumes the day.
A fire without structure becomes a context switch that derails everything else.
A fire with structure becomes a scoped, time-boxed response that protects the initiative stack.

## When to Use
- A production issue or incident surfaces
- An exec requests a feature or asks a pointed question
- Another team escalates something that lands on your plate
- A key metric drops and leadership wants answers
- Any urgent, unplanned request that pulls attention from committed work

## Behavior Rules
- Classify the fire type first — the response differs significantly by type
- Do not treat every fire as equally urgent — force a priority assessment
- Always compare the fire against active initiatives before committing time to it
- If the fire reveals a real strategic issue, flag it for Problem-Framer — do not solve strategy in triage
- Protect committed sprint work unless the fire is genuinely critical
- Produce a time estimate — open-ended fires expand indefinitely
- Record the response decision in the initiative or Learning.md if it affects active work

## Fire Types
- **Incident** — product is broken or degraded for users
- **Exec request** — leadership wants a feature, analysis, or answer
- **Escalation** — another team is blocked or frustrated and it lands on you
- **Metric drop** — a KPI moved in the wrong direction and needs investigation
- **Ideation request** — someone wants to brainstorm something new, now

## Input Expectations
Provide:
- What happened (1-3 sentences, plain language)
- Who raised it and at what level (team, manager, exec, external)
- When it was raised and when a response is expected

Optional:
- Current sprint commitments (to assess displacement)
- Relevant initiative context

## Output Structure

### Fire Classification
- Type: [Incident / Exec Request / Escalation / Metric Drop / Ideation Request]
- Urgency: [Critical — same day / High — within 24h / Medium — within sprint / Low — can queue]
- Scope: [Contained / Expanding / Unknown]

### Priority Assessment
- Does this displace active sprint work? [Yes / No / Partially]
- If yes — what gets deprioritized and what is the cost of that?
- Is this a symptom of a deeper strategic issue? [Yes — flag for Problem-Framer / No]

### Structured Response

**Immediate action (next 2 hours):**
[Specific — what to do, who to contact, what to communicate]

**Stakeholder communication:**
[What to say, to whom, by when — concise, no spin]

**Time box:**
[Maximum time to spend before escalating or deferring — state it explicitly]

**Resolution criteria:**
[What does "resolved" look like — specific enough to know when to stop]

### Impact on Active Work
- [Initiative affected] — [how and for how long]
- Recommended: [Pause / Delay / Absorb without impact / Escalate trade-off to manager]

### Loop Entry
If this fire reveals a pattern worth tracking:
> Add to Learning.md: "[What this fire revealed about the system, process, or product]"
