# Skill: Hiring Brief Builder

## Purpose
Build a PM job brief that is linked directly to current strategy — not a generic job description.
Define the scope, success criteria, and must-have profile for a specific open role.

## When to Use
- Opening a new PM headcount
- Backfilling a departing PM
- Expanding a PM's scope into a new bet and need to define the role clearly
- Briefing a recruiter or hiring manager on what "good" looks like for this specific role

## Behavior Rules
- Every role must link to a bet. A PM hired without a clear strategic mandate will drift.
- Must-haves are things that disqualify a candidate if missing. Keep the list short (3-4 max) or it becomes a wish list.
- Success in 90 days must be concrete and measurable — not "ramped up and contributing."
- Do not write the job brief around a specific candidate. Write it around the role's strategic need.

## Input Expectations
Provide:
- Role level: APM / PM / Senior PM / Staff PM
- Bet or product area this role owns
- Why this headcount is needed now (growth, backfill, new bet)
- Any known constraints (seniority budget, timeline, specific domain requirement)

## Output Structure

### Hiring Brief: [Role Title]

**Level:** [APM / PM / Senior PM / Staff PM]
**Bet owned:** [Bet name and number]
**Hiring reason:** [Growth / Backfill / New bet]
**Target start:** [Quarter]

---

#### Why This Role, Why Now

> [2-3 sentences — what strategic gap this hire closes, and what happens if it isn't filled]

---

#### Scope

This PM will own:
- [Product area / customer segment / capability]
- [Initiatives in flight they will inherit or launch]
- [Stakeholders they will own the relationship with]

They will NOT own:
- [Adjacent areas that could cause scope confusion — draw the line explicitly]

---

#### Success in 90 Days

By the end of the first 90 days, this PM should have:
1. [Concrete milestone — not "met the team"]
2. [Concrete milestone]
3. [Concrete milestone]

---

#### Must-Haves (Disqualifiers if Missing)

- [Specific experience, domain knowledge, or demonstrated capability]
- [...]
- [...] (maximum 4)

#### Strong Signal (Differentiators, Not Requirements)

- [Nice to have — what makes a good candidate great for this specific bet]

---

#### Interview Approach

Suggested focus areas:
1. [Area] — what to probe for — [suggested question or exercise]
2. [Area] — what to probe for — [...]
3. [Area] — what to probe for — [...]

---

#### Recruiter Brief (1 paragraph)

> [Plain language description of the role and ideal profile — written to be forwarded directly to a recruiter or shared in a job posting]
