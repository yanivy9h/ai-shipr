# Skill: PM Performance Review

## Purpose
Structure a performance conversation with a direct report.
Ground it in observable behavior and outcomes — not impressions.
Produce a framework the lead can use to deliver the conversation clearly and hold it if challenged.

## When to Use
- Formal review cycle (mid-year, annual)
- When informal feedback hasn't produced change and a structured conversation is needed
- When a PM is being considered for promotion, scope expansion, or role change
- When a PM's performance requires documentation

## Behavior Rules
- Every observation must be behavioral and specific — not a character judgment
- Performance must be evaluated against the role's expectations, not against other PMs
- Strengths must be genuine — a strengths section that is filler undermines the whole conversation
- Promotion readiness requires evidence, not tenure
- If there is not enough evidence to support a rating, say so and list what would be needed

## Input Expectations
Provide:
- PM name and role
- Review type: formal review / promotion assessment / improvement conversation
- Time period being reviewed
- Key initiatives and outcomes during this period
- Any specific situations worth referencing (positive or developmental)

## Output Structure

### Performance Summary: [PM Name] — [Period]

**Role:** [Title]
**Review type:** [Formal / Promotion / Improvement]

---

#### Strengths — What to Build On

For each strength:
- **[Strength area]:** [Specific observation with example] — [Why it matters for the role]

Minimum 2, maximum 4. Must be real and evidenced.

---

#### Development Areas — What to Address

For each area:
- **[Area]:** [Specific observation with example] — [Expected standard for this role] — [What change looks like]

---

#### Outcomes Assessment

| Initiative | Expected Outcome | Actual Outcome | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------------|---------------|------------|
| [Initiative] | [Metric / milestone] | [What happened] | Met / Partial / Missed |

---

#### Overall Assessment

**Summary:** [2-3 sentences — honest, grounded, specific]

**Promotion readiness** (if applicable):
- Ready: [Y/N] — Evidence: [specific]
- Gap to next level: [what is missing and why it matters]

---

#### Conversation Framework

How to open:
> "[Opening line that sets tone without being ambiguous about purpose]"

Anticipated response and how to hold the position:
- If they push back on [specific point]: [how to respond without retreating or escalating]

Closing ask:
> "[What you want them to leave the conversation committed to]"

Follow-up: schedule a check-in in [N] weeks to review [specific behavior or outcome].
