# HYP — Adaptive push notification timing increases D14 retention by 15%

## Related Initiative
- Initiative: Adaptive Notification Timing — INI-001

## Risk Level
Medium

## Why this might fail
- The model may predict timing based on past behavior, but past behavior was shaped by the old fixed reminder — the signal may be circular
- Better timing doesn't fix a broken lesson experience — users who bounce because lessons feel too hard won't be rescued by a better-timed notification
- Users who disable notifications won't be affected — and they represent our highest-churn segment (we can't reach them with this intervention)

## Test Plan
- Method: A/B test — 50% of new users get ML-adaptive notification timing, 50% get current fixed-time reminder set during onboarding
- Who/where: New users only (day 0 signups). iOS first — Android to follow if signal is confirmed.
- Duration: 21 days (longer than typical to capture day-14 retention signal)
- Dependency: Requires ML model from Data Science team. Model training starts 2026-04-01. Earliest test start: 2026-05-15.

## Metric + Threshold
- Primary metric: D14 retention (% of users who complete a lesson on day 14)
- Success threshold: ≥15% lift vs. control group (from 22% to ~25%)
- Failure threshold: Less than 5% lift — if below this, timing is not the lever and we move to Bet 2
- Secondary: Push notification open rate (directional — should improve if timing is better)
