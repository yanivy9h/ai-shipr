# HYP — A free streak repair option at first loss reduces day-of-loss churn by 30%

## Related Initiative
- Initiative: Streak Repair at First Loss — INI-002

## Risk Level
High

## Why this might fail
- Free streak repair may devalue Streak Freeze (a paid feature) and reduce Super Duolingo conversion — the risk is real and Finance will push back
- Users may lose their streak intentionally if they know repair is free — gamification integrity breaks down
- The churn might not be emotional — users who quit after losing a streak may simply have no motivation to return regardless of the repair option
- First-loss timing is hard to catch: users may not open the app on the day they lose their streak — the repair offer has to be surfaced the next time they return, not at the moment of loss

## Test Plan
- Method: A/B test — 50% of users who lose their first streak see a new repair screen (one-time free repair, clearly labelled "first time only"). 50% see the current experience (broken flame, Streak Freeze upsell).
- Who/where: Users losing their first streak only. iOS and Android simultaneously. Free users only — Super Duolingo users already have access to Streak Freeze.
- Duration: 30 days (need enough first-loss events and sufficient follow-up time to measure D30 retention impact)

## Metric + Threshold
- Primary metric: % of first-streak-loss users who return and complete a lesson within 7 days
- Current baseline: 62% do not return (38% churn rate at first loss)
- Success threshold: Return rate improves from 62% to at least 75% (churn drops from 38% to 25%)
- Failure threshold: Less than 5% improvement in return rate
- Secondary: Super Duolingo conversion rate in treatment vs. control — monitor for cannibalization signal
