# Initiative: Adaptive Notification Timing

## Strategic Link
- Strategic-Bets: Bet 1 — Adaptive notification timing
- Why this bet now: Push opt-in rate dropped 6 points after iOS permission changes in 2025. Fixed-time reminders are generating fatigue — users are dismissing reminders at the moment they were set but the habit hasn't formed yet. Behavior data shows 60% of users engage at a time different from their chosen reminder. Adaptive timing is the highest-leverage fix that doesn't require a product redesign.

## Outcome (must be measurable)
- Metric: D14 retention
- Desired change: From 22% to at least 25% in treatment group (≥15% relative lift)
- Time horizon: Measure at day 14 from signup, test cohort from 2026-05-15

## Linked Hypotheses
- HYP-001 — Adaptive notification timing increases D14 retention by 15%

## Decision Needed?
Yes
- Open question: Should adaptive timing be opt-in (user can revert to fixed time) or automatic (system controls it silently)? Opt-in preserves user control; automatic gives us cleaner signal. Decision needed before engineering scopes the experiment.

## Open Questions
- If the model underperforms in the first 7 days of training, do we delay the test or ship with a simpler rule-based heuristic (e.g., "send notification at the time of last 3-day average engagement")?
- Should we test adaptive timing on re-engaged users (returning after a gap) separately from new users?
