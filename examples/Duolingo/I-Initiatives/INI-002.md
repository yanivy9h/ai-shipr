# Initiative: Streak Repair at First Loss

## Strategic Link
- Strategic-Bets: Bet 2 — Streak repair at first loss
- Why this bet now: 38% of users who lose their first streak never return. The current experience shows a broken flame with no recovery path for free users who have no Gems. This is our most expensive churn moment — it happens to every user who builds a streak, and we're doing nothing to recover them.

## Outcome (must be measurable)
- Metric: % of first-streak-loss users who return and complete a lesson within 7 days
- Desired change: Return rate from 62% to at least 75% (churn rate drops from 38% to 25%)
- Time horizon: 30-day test with day-7 early read

## Linked Hypotheses
- HYP-002 — A free streak repair option at first loss reduces day-of-loss churn by 30%

## Stakeholders
- James Park (Finance) — Streak Freeze is a paid feature. Free repair must not cannibalize it. James needs a revenue risk analysis before this goes to experiment. Flag: needs meeting before sprint starts.
- Aisha Okafor (iOS Engineering Lead) — backend streak service is legacy. She flagged 2-sprint estimate and regression risk. Need her in planning.
- Marcus Webb (Legal/Policy) — "free, first time only" framing needs policy review. Some markets may require different language.

## Decision Needed?
Yes
- Decision 1: Free repair for free users only, or include Super Duolingo users too? (Super Duolingo users already have Streak Freeze — giving them a second repair channel may confuse the product.)
- Decision 2: Is "first time only" enforced forever, or can it reset annually? Forever enforcement is simpler but may feel punitive to long-term users.

## Open Questions
- If the streak repair is free once, do we offer a discounted Gem purchase as the second option immediately after? Could this convert a churn moment into a monetization moment.
- What happens if the user loses their first streak and doesn't open the app for 3 days? Is the repair offer still available when they return, or does it expire?
