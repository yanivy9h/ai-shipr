# EXP-001 — Streak Repair at First Loss A/B Test

## Linked Hypothesis
- HYP-002 — A free streak repair option at first loss reduces day-of-loss churn by 30%

## Linked Initiative
- INI-002 — Streak Repair at First Loss

## Status
Planned

## Method
A/B test — split applied at the moment a user loses their first streak
- **Treatment (50%)**: Streak repair screen replaces the broken-flame state. "First time only" free repair offered. Single tap to restore streak. Followed by: lesson prompt and optional Gems upsell.
- **Control (50%)**: Current experience — broken flame icon, Streak Freeze upsell (requires Gems), no free repair path.

Experiment applies to: Free users only. Users who have lost their first streak (streak was ≥1 day, now shows 0). iOS and Android simultaneously.

## Experiment Window
- Planned start: 2026-04-14 (pending Finance alignment by 2026-04-07)
- End date: 2026-05-14 (30 days)
- Early read: 2026-04-21 (day 7 — check return rate signal and flag if Streak Freeze conversion drops sharply)

## Who's in the Test
- Free users only (Super Duolingo users excluded — they have Streak Freeze already)
- First-streak-loss events only — users who have previously lost a streak are excluded
- Minimum 500 users per arm to reach statistical confidence — estimated 4–5 days to reach threshold based on current streak-loss volume

## Primary Metric
- **Return rate after streak loss**: % of users who open the app and complete a lesson within 7 days of losing their first streak
- **Baseline (control)**: 62%
- **Success threshold**: ≥75% in treatment arm
- **Failure threshold**: <65% — if below this, free repair is not addressing the real churn driver

## Secondary Metrics (directional only)
- Super Duolingo conversion rate — treatment vs. control (monitoring for cannibalization)
- Gems purchased immediately after repair — does the repair moment create a monetization opportunity?
- Day-30 retention — does recovering first-streak-loss users produce lasting retention or just short-term return?

## Risks Being Watched
- **Streak Freeze revenue drop**: If treatment group converts to Super Duolingo at a meaningfully lower rate, this becomes a Finance escalation. Monitoring weekly.
- **Abuse pattern**: Are users who receive the free repair losing streaks more frequently in the following weeks? Would signal that the repair is being "used as a safety net" rather than recovering genuine churn.
- **Novelty effect**: First-week return rate may be inflated by curiosity about the new experience — day-30 data is the more reliable signal.

## Owner
Maya Chen (PM). Aisha Okafor (iOS lead) owns backend streak integration. James Park (Finance) reviewing revenue risk weekly.

## Next Decision Point
- If day-7 reads ≥70% return rate (treatment): Continue to day 30, begin planning full rollout.
- If day-7 reads 65–70%: Extend test, investigate whether timing of the repair offer (immediate vs. next-open) changes the result.
- If day-7 reads <65% OR Streak Freeze conversion drops >10%: Pause test, escalate to Finance and Head of Product for decision.
