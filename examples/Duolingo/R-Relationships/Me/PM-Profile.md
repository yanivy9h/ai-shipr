# PM Profile

## My role in the team
- Title: Senior Product Manager, Learner Engagement
- Responsibilities: Own the retention metrics (D14, D30), run discovery on habit formation and churn moments, write experiment briefs, align Data Science and Engineering on initiative sequencing
- Decision ownership: Experiment design, feature scope, prioritization within the Learner Engagement track. Escalate to Head of Product (Raj) on decisions that touch monetization or cross-team dependencies.
- Interfaces with: Raj Krishnamurthy (Head of Product) weekly, Priya Patel (Data Science) biweekly for experiment readouts, Aisha Okafor (iOS Eng Lead) in sprint, James Park (Finance) on any features that touch monetization

## My biggest challenge right now
D14 retention is stuck at 22% and has not moved in three quarters. I have two credible bets — notification timing and streak repair — but I can only run one experiment at a time without polluting the signal. The timing bet has a dependency on a Data Science model that won't be ready until May. The streak repair bet is blocked by Finance alignment. Both are stalled for different reasons and I'm running out of Q2.

## Where I spend most of my time
- Stakeholder management: Finance wants revenue protection, Data Science is capacity-constrained, Engineering is worried about the legacy streak service — alignment takes longer than it should
- Experiment design: Writing briefs that are specific enough to be actionable and defensible enough to survive Finance review
- Data analysis: Reading Mixpanel dashboards daily, pulling cohort retention curves manually — no automated dashboard exists yet for my team's metrics

## Where I want leverage from AI-SHIPR
- Faster hypothesis framing: I need to go from "we think notification timing matters" to a falsifiable hypothesis with a defined failure threshold faster than I do today
- Stakeholder-ready decision memos: When I bring something to James at Finance, I need a structured argument — not bullet points. First drafts take me too long.
- Weekly review structure: I lose track of what's stalled vs. what's moving. I need a system that surfaces the blockers before my Monday standup.

## My operating preference
Evidence-first, bias toward action once evidence threshold is met. I distrust vanity metrics and committee decisions. I'd rather run a fast experiment with a clear failure threshold than debate the right answer for three sprints.
