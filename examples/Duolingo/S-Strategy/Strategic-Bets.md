# Strategic Bets (max 3)

Documents the 1–3 areas where the team is placing its bets this period.
The most-read file in the system — sprint planning, initiative review, OKRs, and business cases all reference it.
Update quarterly or when a bet is confirmed or killed.

---

## Bet 1 — Adaptive notification timing
- Why this matters: Our push notifications go out at a fixed time chosen during onboarding. Usage data shows that 60% of users who set "7pm" as their reminder actually open the app at a different time — they're on the subway at 8am, or watching TV at 10pm. We're sending reminders when users are unavailable and missing the windows when they'd actually open the app. Personalized timing should improve opt-in rate and reduce notification fatigue-driven churn.
- Who it serves: Maya the Casual Learner — she keeps missing the 7pm reminder because she's cooking dinner. She'd open it at 9pm with her tea, if we asked at the right moment.
- What success looks like: Push notification opt-in rate improves from 54% to 65%. D14 retention improves by at least 15% in the treatment group.
- Time horizon: Q2 2026

## Bet 2 — Streak repair at first loss
- Why this matters: When a user loses their streak for the first time, 38% of them never return. The emotional cost of losing a streak — especially early in the habit — is higher than the cost of starting the app for the first time. The current experience shows a broken flame and asks users to buy a Streak Freeze. For new users with no Gems, there's no repair path. We're converting a recoverable moment into a churn event.
- Who it serves: The Motivated Returnee — has tried Duolingo before and quit. Losing a 6-day streak would confirm "I'm not the kind of person who sticks with this."
- What success looks like: D30 retention improves among users who lose their first streak. Churn at day-of-streak-loss drops from 38% to 25%.
- Time horizon: Q2–Q3 2026

## Bet 3 — Social accountability loop
- Why this matters: Duolingo's Friends Leaderboard exists but drives anxiety, not commitment. Users compete, fall behind, and disengage. What we don't have is quiet accountability — a small group of known friends where learning is visible but not ranked. Users who share Duolingo with a real-world contact have 2.3x higher 30-day retention (internal data, 2025 cohort analysis). We haven't productized that signal.
- Who it serves: The Streak Purist — has an existing streak, would love to see a friend's streak and feel validated. The Motivated Returnee — accountability to one known person beats a leaderboard of strangers.
- What success looks like: 20% of new users add at least one friend within 7 days of signup. 30-day retention of users with 1+ friend is ≥2x that of users with no friends.
- Time horizon: Q3 2026
