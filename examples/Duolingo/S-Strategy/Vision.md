# Vision

Defines the product in one sentence and sets the direction for where it's going.
Agents read this to anchor all output to what you're actually building.
Update at a product pivot or quarterly.

---

## Product in one sentence
Duolingo makes language learning a daily habit — not a chore — through streaks, progress feedback, and a lesson experience short enough to fit between two subway stops.

## Vision (2–5 lines)
Be the first app people open in the morning and the last one they close at night — not because it demands attention, but because they genuinely want to keep their streak alive.

The learner engagement team owns the habit loop: what brings people back on day 2, day 7, and day 30. Our job is not content — it's the pull mechanism that makes content matter. Every feature we build should make the next session feel more inevitable than the last one.

## What we will NOT do
- Build long-form courses or replace structured language programs (that's Duolingo for Schools, a different team)
- Compete on grammar depth or linguistic accuracy — comprehensible progress beats technical correctness for habit formation
- Compromise lesson completion speed for content richness — lessons must feel winnable in under 5 minutes
